5 · Origin Story

A 200-year-old network effect. Rebuilt for the quantum era.

FiatBarter isn't a new idea. It's the oldest international settlement network in modern banking — updated with cryptographic guarantees the original never had.

— 1810s · London, Paris, Frankfurt, Vienna, Naples
The Rothschild Courier System
Five Rothschild brothers resided in five European capitals. When a London client needed to pay a Paris counterparty, no gold actually crossed borders. The London brother debited the client; the Paris brother credited the recipient. Balances settled between brothers on their own ledger. Value moved at the speed of couriers — not horses carrying coin on bandit-infested roads.
→
— 2026 · SodsBC FiatBarter
The Protocol
Member banks hold local accounts on SodsBC. Cross-border obligations post as local debits/credits on the chain. Multilateral netting collapses gross flows — typically 94–96%, matching CLS Bank's published FX netting efficiency[22] — to a small net delta that settles through existing SWIFT rails. Post-quantum signatures, sub-second finality, EVM smart contracts, auditable forever.
§ Worked example
US entities owe Singapore entities $1M. Singapore entities owe US entities ~S$1.35M (equiv. $1M). Instead of 2 wire transfers through correspondents with FX spreads and 2-day float, SodsBC debits USD and SGD on-chain locally. Zero FX conversion. Zero correspondent hops. Zero waiting. Net delta (if any) clears through banks' existing rails. Extras beyond the barter — interchange, negotiated bank fees — accrue to the platform and members.
